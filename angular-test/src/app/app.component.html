    • Imports, Declarations And Providers

    1. Imports: is used to makes the exported declarations of other modoles available in the current module
    2. Declarations: is used to declare components, directives, pipes that belongs to the current module. Everything inside declarations knows each other.
    3. Providers: are used to make services and values known to dependency injection. They are added to the root scope and they are injected to other services or directives that have them as dependency.

      Components, Directives, Models, Modules, and Services

    1. Components: are basically classes that interact with the html file of the component, which gets displayed on the browser.
    2. Directives: it exend the scope of html, are responsible for updating the DOM when the state of the model changes.
    3. Models: are used to make services and values known to dependency injection. They are added to the root scope and they are injected to other services or directives that have them as dependency.
    4. Modules: are used to configure the dependency injection, allowing the things defined by the module to be injected elsewhere.
    5. Services: are a great way to share information among classes that don't know each other.
